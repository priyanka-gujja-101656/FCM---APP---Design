04 · The Market Problem

Childcare administration is brokenno one has fixed it end to end

Directors spend hours each month reconciling subsidies in spreadsheets. Teachers log activities on phones that were not built for one-handed use. Parents pay tuition across multiple apps. Everyone tolerates it because the alternatives are worse.

Director pain

Subsidy reconciliation eats 6+ hours per month

CCAP/DHS claims vs. attendance is manual and error-prone. A single missed mismatch costs $100–$500.

Teacher pain

Daily logging eats 30–45 minutes per day

20+ activities logged daily. At 32–50 seconds per entry, that's up to 45 minutes of classroom time lost. Most teachers log in batches after hours.

Parent pain

The "is my kid okay?" loop is leaky

Parents check the app 6–10 times a day. Slow updates and fragmented payment flows are the #1 reason families switch centers.

The structural insight: these three pains share a single root causefragmented data and incomplete feature coverage on every existing product. Fix the data spine and the workflow, and all three roles win at the same time.

06 · The Design Direction

A ground-up redesignbuilt for how people actually work

The current product works. The new design makes it feel effortless. Every decision in the revamp traces back to one principle: the software should adapt to the user, not the other way around.

1

Design principle

Native & modern

Designed for how people actually use phones and tabletsnot a desktop app shrunk to fit a screen. Gestures, spacing, and tap targets built for real classroom conditions.

2

Design principle

Intuitive from day one

Directors and teachers get started without a training session. Every flow is scoped to a single task. The most common action is always one tap away.

3

Design principle

Customizable to each center

Centers configure the app to match their workflowclassrooms, schedules, activity typeswithout calling support. No two centers are the same; the product flexes to fit.

4

Design principle

AI that reduces friction

The AI assistant surfaces the right information at the right momentdraft a daily report, flag a subsidy gap, remind about an expiring certification. It does the repetitive work so people can focus on the kids.

5

Design principle

Built for teachers first

If the logging experience isn't fast and frictionless, nothing else works. One-handed, offline-capable, under 10 seconds. The teacher experience is the foundation the whole product stands on.

+

Design principle

Features that earn trust

Privacy controls parents can see and manage. Consent that follows the child across every touchpoint. COPPA isn't a legal footnote, it's a visible product feature that builds confidence in FCM.

The shift: existing childcare software was built to store data. The FCM revamp is built to reduce decisions for the director, the teacher, and eventually the parent. Less time in the app means more time with the kids, which is the entire point.

What the journey work surfaced

Dana's morning

The 11:14 AM subsidy moment

Dana arrives at 7:30 AM. By 11:14 AM the AI assistant has surfaced four things: an overdue family, an expiring certification, a subsidy gap, an inbox question. The dashboard's job is to land her on the highest-leverage decision in under 10 seconds.

Tara's classroom

The under-10-second activity log

Tara logs a meal for 8 children one-handed while supervising play. Current tools take 35–50 seconds per entry. FCM targets under 10 by collapsing student, activity, and photo into one screen with smart defaults.

Cross-role

Consent that follows the child

Privacy is not a settings page, it is a feature. Parents adjust what data is shared. The toggle is honored at every capture point: camera, daily report, family directory. It follows the child without re-asking at each touchpoint.

Adoption cascade: if Tara stops logging, parents get no updates, and the center loses its best retention tool. Tara's adoption is the keystone; everything else depends on it.
08 · Five Flows That Win

One differentiating flow per pillar

If FCM does these five things measurably better than any competitor, the rest of the product is leverage.

1

Subsidy reconciliation

Dana · once a month · 6+ hours saved
The AI assistant scans attendance against claims and surfaces mismatches as per-child cards: Accept, Edit, or Decline. No spreadsheet. No manual math.
Beats Procare on UX. Beats Brightwheel on existing at all.
2

Under-10-second activity log

Tara · 20+ times per day · 30 minutes per day saved
One-handed, offline-capable, smart defaults. Student, activity type, done. Current tools average 32–50 seconds per entry. FCM targets under 10.
The adoption surface every competitor loses on. FCM wins this or doesn't win.
3

AI daily-report compose and approve

Tara · end of day · 12 reports in under 3 minutes
The AI assistant drafts a per-child summary from the day's log. Tara reviews, edits, approves in batch. Consistent, warm reports without the manual writing time.
Lillio's home turf. Match this and FCM takes their primary differentiator off the table.
4

Visible consent management

Priya · once at enrollment, reviewable anytime
Parents see exactly what is captured about their child and control it with a toggle. Changes are enforced at every capture point. COPPA is not a footer link.
No competitor does this. A Nelnet-grade trust position that turns privacy into a reason to choose FCM.
11 · Strategic Framework

Strong on five pillars at onceno single moat, mutual reinforcement

A competitor who beats FCM on any one pillar still loses on the other four. That is the strategic shape.

1

Billing & subsidy

Money that just works

Native CCAP/DHS reconciliation, attendance-based billing, refunds and credits done right. The loudest unmet need in the categoryand the clearest moat against Brightwheel.

2

Teacher loop

Logging that gets used

Daily logging in under 10 seconds, one-handed, offline-capable, AI-drafted. The adoption surface every competitor loses on. Get this right or nothing else matters.

3

Center customization

Workflows that fit the center

Rate plans, classroom configurations, custom forms, and workflow options that directors configure without calling support. Every center runs differentlythe product must flex to match.

4

Pricing

Flat, predictable bundle

Per-center, never per-child. Enrollment-driven rate plans, signed proposals, automated billing. Neutralizes the loudest customer complaint about every competitor in the category.

5

Trust

COPPA, made visible

Privacy-by-design, consent surfaced as a product feature, Nelnet-grade security posture as a buyer promise. The category's third rail becomes FCM's visible differentiator.

+

Accelerator

FACTS SIS integration

For the install base: accelerates billing, eliminates double-entry, seeds AI with clean data. Not a prerequisite. FCM must win standalone on merit too. But the beachhead is near-zero CAC.

Next Steps

What we've done. What we test. How we build.

Where we are

Prototype built for Admin + Teacher

We have a working prototype across the Director and Teacher surfaces. The five critical flows are designed. The next step is getting this in front of real users.

What to test

Usability testing with real clients

Run structured sessions with actual directors and teachers. Validate the under-10-second logging claim, the subsidy flow, and whether the AI assistant suggestions feel trustworthy, before a line of production code is written.

How to build

One pillar at a time, in priority order

Start with the teacher loop, the keystone. Layer in subsidy (single state first). Then center customization. Each stage is validated before the next begins.
