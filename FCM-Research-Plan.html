FACTS Childcare Management (FCM) · 0→1 Research Plan & Assumption Map · June 2026

De-risking the FCM bet before we build

A sequenced discovery plan and a ranked map of the assumptions FCM is built on. The goal of 0→1 research isn't to validate the roadmap — it's to find out, fast and cheaply, whether the problem is real, whose it is, and which one flow we cannot get wrong.

The core bet in one line: FACTS-SIS schools with attached early-learning programs feel enough operational pain — especially around billing/subsidy and daily family communication — to adopt a second FACTS module rather than bolt on a standalone competitor. Everything below exists to confirm or kill that.

What this research must answer

Primary questions

  • Is the pain real & ranked? What actually hurts most for FACTS-SIS centers — billing, comms, compliance, staffing — and in what order?
  • Whose problem is it? Who buys (director), who lives in it daily (teacher), who judges it (parent) — and where do their needs collide?
  • What triggers a switch/adopt? What event makes a center say "yes" to another module vs. gluing on Brightwheel?
  • Is integration the real wedge? Do centers value SIS-native billing/zero-double-entry enough to choose FCM for it?

Success criteria for the research itself

  • Top 3 pains ranked with evidence, not opinion
  • A clear primary persona + the buyer/user split named
  • The one make-or-break flow identified and prototyped
  • Baseline metrics captured (so impact is provable later)
  • Go / refine / pivot recommendation the team trusts

The plan — sequenced

1

Internal mining & framing · week 1 · no fieldwork needed

Start with what FACTS already knows before talking to anyone outside.

  • Pull install-base data: how many SIS schools have eligible (Premier + shared tax ID) early-learning programs? Sizes the market and de-risks a MED assumption for free.
  • Mine existing support tickets, churn reasons, and sales-loss notes for childcare-adjacent pain.
  • Lock the riskiest-assumptions list (above) with PM + eng as the shared target.
2

Generative discovery — go to the centers · weeks 2–4

Observed behavior over stated preference. This is where the HIGH-risk assumptions get tested.

  • 8–12 contextual inquiries in real FACTS-SIS centers — watch a teacher do morning check-in + logging, watch a director run billing/subsidy reconciliation.
  • Interviews across all three roles (director, teacher, parent) to map needs and where they collide.
  • Capture baseline metrics live: minutes per daily report, time to reconcile a claim, current tools in use, % parents paying on time.
  • Probe the switching trigger — what event would make them adopt or rip out a tool.
3

Synthesis → shared artifacts · week 5

Turn raw sessions into things the whole team builds from.

  • Ranked pain/opportunity list with evidence and frequency.
  • Validated personas (replacing the provisional ones) + the buyer/user split.
  • Journey maps of the two make-or-break flows with pain/emotion peaks marked.
  • A clear call on which single flow is riskiest to get wrong.
4

Concept & usability testing — the riskiest flow · weeks 6–8

Don't test everything; test the thing that sinks the product if wrong.

  • Low-fi prototype of the SIS-native billing/subsidy flow (the moat) and the teacher daily-logging loop (the adoption killer).
  • 5–8 task-based usability sessions per flow; measure stumble points, completion, comprehension.
  • Test the integration value prop directly: does "it already knows your students/billing" change their reaction?
5

Decision & baseline lock · week 9

  • Go / refine / pivot recommendation tied back to the assumption map — which HIGH risks cleared, which didn't.
  • Locked baseline metrics so post-launch impact is measurable, not anecdotal.
  • Prioritized opportunity backlog handed to PM/design.
